## 1. Purpose
Control the identification, ownership, use, classification, labeling, transfer, retention, deletion, and return of information and associated assets.

## 2. Scope
Applies to information assets in Microsoft 365, Azure, GitLab, Jira, Confluence, laptops, removable media, shipment processes, and paper records where permitted.

## 3. Authority and Compliance
This policy is issued under the authority of the CTO. Compliance with this document is mandatory for all personnel, contractors, and third parties within its scope. Non-compliance shall be addressed through management action, corrective action, contractual enforcement, or disciplinary measures as applicable.

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities
- The CTO is the document approver and provides executive authorization for this document.
- The Head of IT is the document owner and is responsible for implementation, maintenance, and periodic review.
- Managers shall ensure that personnel within their areas of responsibility comply with the requirements of this document.
- All workers, contractors, and third parties in scope shall comply with the requirements defined in this document.

## 5. Policy Requirements
### 5.1 Asset and Ownership Requirements
- Information and technology assets shall be inventoried, assigned an owner, and reviewed at least annually.
- Owners shall define acceptable use, handling requirements, retention expectations, and recovery requirements for assets within their responsibility.

### 5.2 Acceptable Use and Handling
- Workers shall use company assets only for authorized business purposes and shall not disable approved security controls.
- Printing company information is prohibited unless explicitly approved by the Head of IT for a time-bound business need.
- Information shall be classified at least as Public, Internal, Confidential, or Restricted and handled according to the classification scheme.
- Labels or equivalent metadata shall be applied to documents, repositories, systems, or workspaces containing Confidential or Restricted information.
- Transfers of sensitive information shall use approved encrypted channels and company-managed services.

### 5.3 Media, Retention and Disposal
- Use of removable media is prohibited by default and requires documented business approval.
- Business records shall be retained, protected from unauthorized change, and disposed of securely when no longer needed.
- Information deletion shall be performed using vendor-supported purge features or sanitization methods appropriate to the medium.
- Company assets shall be returned or securely destroyed when no longer required or upon termination.

### 5.4 Mandatory Asset and Information Handling Controls
- An inventory shall be maintained for company-managed endpoints, datacenter equipment, critical software platforms, repositories, and information assets requiring defined ownership.
- Each asset owner shall define classification, retention, recovery, and approved-use requirements for assets under their control.
- Company information shall not be stored in unapproved personal services, unencrypted media, or unmanaged collaboration channels.

### 5.5 Transfer, Retention and Return Requirements
- Sensitive information transfers shall use approved business channels with encryption in transit and recipient verification where appropriate.
- Records retention periods and disposal methods shall be defined for security, employment, contractual, and business records.
- Asset return shall be completed promptly upon termination, replacement, or reassignment and verified through documented chain of custody.
