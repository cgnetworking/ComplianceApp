## 1. Purpose
This Statement of Applicability defines the applicability status of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A controls for the organization's Information Security Management System and identifies the primary means by which applicable controls are implemented.

## 2. Scope Summary
The ISMS covers the fully remote software company workforce, company-managed laptops, BYOD mobile access through managed app protection, Microsoft 365, Azure, GitLab, Jira, Confluence, CrowdStrike, Intune, Fortigate, the office used for shipments, and company-owned equipment installed in a third-party colocation facility.

## 3. Authority and Approval
This document is approved by the CTO and maintained by the Head of IT. It shall be used together with the risk assessment and treatment methodology to support risk treatment decisions, internal audit, management review, and certification activities.

## 4. Applicability Basis
- Applicable controls are implemented through the document set in this package and supporting technical or supplier controls.
- Shared controls rely partly on external providers such as the colocation facility, Azure, or certified SaaS vendors, with internal governance retained by the organization.
- Excluded controls are not applicable to the current scope and risk profile and must be re-evaluated if the operating model changes.

## 5. Exclusions
- **7.6 Working in secure areas**: excluded because the organization does not operate its own secure areas. Colocation secure areas are provider-managed and addressed through supplier assurance and visitor procedures.
- **8.11 Data masking**: excluded because live customer or similarly sensitive production datasets are not used in non-production workflows. Synthetic or sanitized information is used instead.
- **8.30 Outsourced development**: excluded because in-scope software development is performed internally. The third-party manufacturer is managed as a supplier for hardware production, not outsourced software development.
