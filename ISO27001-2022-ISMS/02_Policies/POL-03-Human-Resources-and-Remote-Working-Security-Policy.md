## 1. Purpose
Define personnel security, onboarding, training, confidentiality, disciplinary expectations, event reporting, and remote working requirements.

## 2. Scope
Applies to employees, contractors, interns, temporary workers, and other users granted access to company information or systems.

## 3. Authority and Compliance
This policy is issued under the authority of the CTO. Compliance with this document is mandatory for all personnel, contractors, and third parties within its scope. Non-compliance shall be addressed through management action, corrective action, contractual enforcement, or disciplinary measures as applicable.

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities
- The CTO is the document approver and provides executive authorization for this document.
- The Head of IT is the document owner and is responsible for implementation, maintenance, and periodic review.
- Managers shall ensure that personnel within their areas of responsibility comply with the requirements of this document.
- All workers, contractors, and third parties in scope shall comply with the requirements defined in this document.

## 5. Policy Requirements
### 5.1 Personnel Security
- Screening shall be performed before granting access in accordance with legal requirements and role sensitivity.
- Employment agreements and contractor terms shall define security responsibilities, confidentiality obligations, acceptable use, and return of assets.
- All personnel shall complete security awareness training at onboarding and at least annually thereafter.

### 5.2 Remote Working
- Remote work shall be performed only on company-managed laptops or approved virtual environments.
- Home workspaces shall protect screens from casual observation, use full-disk encryption, and lock automatically when unattended.
- BYOD mobile access shall be restricted to approved mobile applications protected by app protection policies and conditional access.
- Workers shall report suspected security events immediately through the documented incident reporting channels.

### 5.3 Employment Change and Exit
- Managers and IT shall coordinate role changes, access updates, and terminations on or before the effective date.
- Disciplinary action may be taken for intentional or negligent violations of security policy.
- Confidentiality obligations shall survive termination where contractually required.

### 5.4 Personnel Lifecycle Controls
- Background screening depth shall align with role sensitivity, access level, and applicable law.
- Employment terms shall require confidentiality, acceptable use, event reporting, protection of company assets, and cooperation during investigations.
- Training shall cover phishing, remote-working risks, data handling, incident reporting, and secure use of collaboration and development platforms.

### 5.5 Remote Working Mandatory Requirements
- Remote workers shall use secure home networks, maintain updated systems, prevent unauthorized observation of screens, and store company assets securely when not in use.
- Printing and local paper storage are prohibited unless explicitly authorized and controlled.
- Lost, stolen, or compromised devices, accounts, or credentials shall be reported immediately.
