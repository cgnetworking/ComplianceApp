## 1. Purpose
Define expectations for preparing for, identifying, assessing, responding to, learning from, and preserving evidence for information security incidents.

## 2. Scope
Applies to suspected or confirmed security events involving people, endpoints, cloud services, code repositories, the colocation environment, supplier services, and communications platforms.

## 3. Authority and Compliance
This policy is issued under the authority of the CTO. Compliance with this document is mandatory for all personnel, contractors, and third parties within its scope. Non-compliance shall be addressed through management action, corrective action, contractual enforcement, or disciplinary measures as applicable.

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities
- The CTO is the document approver and provides executive authorization for this document.
- The Head of IT is the document owner and is responsible for implementation, maintenance, and periodic review.
- Managers shall ensure that personnel within their areas of responsibility comply with the requirements of this document.
- All workers, contractors, and third parties in scope shall comply with the requirements defined in this document.

## 5. Policy Requirements
### 5.1 Response Requirements
- An incident response procedure, severity model, escalation matrix, and communication path shall be maintained and tested.
- Potential security events shall be assessed promptly to determine whether they are incidents requiring coordinated response.
- Incident records shall document timeline, actions taken, impact, containment, eradication, recovery, and closure decisions.

### 5.2 Learning and Evidence
- Lessons learned shall be captured after material incidents or exercises and reflected in risk treatment, procedures, or technical controls.
- Evidence shall be collected, preserved, and handled in a manner that supports internal investigation and potential legal or contractual use.

### 5.3 Incident Management Requirements
- Incident response shall define preparation, detection, triage, containment, eradication, recovery, communication, and closure activities.
- Incident severity criteria shall consider business disruption, data exposure, safety, legal obligations, customer impact, and supplier dependencies.
- Communication responsibilities shall cover internal escalation, executive notification, supplier coordination, and external notifications when required.

### 5.4 Evidence and Lessons Learned Requirements
- Incident evidence shall be collected, preserved, accessed, and transferred in a manner that maintains integrity, chronology, and accountability.
- Post-incident reviews shall identify root causes, control failures, lessons learned, and required corrective or preventive actions.
- Lessons learned from incidents and exercises shall update procedures, training, technical controls, and the risk register.
