## 1. Purpose
Define how information security risks are identified, analyzed, evaluated, treated, accepted, and monitored within the ISMS.

## 2. Scope
Applies to strategic, operational, supplier, physical, personnel, and technical risks affecting the scoped environment.

## 3. Authority and Compliance
This methodology is issued under the authority of the CTO. Compliance with this document is mandatory for all personnel, contractors, and third parties within its scope. Non-compliance shall be addressed through management action, corrective action, contractual enforcement, or disciplinary measures as applicable.

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities
- The CTO is the document approver and provides executive authorization for this document.
- The Head of IT is the document owner and is responsible for implementation, maintenance, and periodic review.
- Managers shall ensure that personnel within their areas of responsibility comply with the requirements of this document.
- All workers, contractors, and third parties in scope shall comply with the requirements defined in this document.

## 5. Methodology Requirements
### 5.1 Method
- Risks shall be identified for assets, business processes, suppliers, facilities, software development, remote working, and technology services.
- Likelihood and impact shall each be rated on a five-point scale. Overall risk shall be determined by multiplying likelihood and impact.
- Impact evaluation shall consider confidentiality, integrity, availability, legal obligations, financial loss, and operational disruption.

### 5.2 Treatment Options
- Risks may be treated by implementing or strengthening controls, transferring risk through contractual or insurance means, avoiding the activity, or formally accepting the residual risk.
- Residual risk acceptance requires documented rationale, owner acknowledgement, and approval by the CTO.
- Annex A controls shall be considered during treatment planning, and the Statement of Applicability shall be updated when applicability changes.

### 5.3 Review Triggers
- The risk register shall be reviewed at least quarterly and whenever a major technology, supplier, office, datacenter, or product change occurs.
- Post-incident lessons learned, audit findings, test failures, and significant exceptions shall feed risk updates.

### 5.4 Risk Scoring and Acceptance Criteria
- Risk acceptance criteria and treatment thresholds shall be defined and consistently applied across business, operational, supplier, physical, and technical risks.
- High and critical risks shall not be accepted without CTO approval and documented business justification.
- Risks dependent on supplier or cloud controls shall identify shared responsibility boundaries and evidence requirements.

### 5.5 Treatment Planning and Monitoring
- Treatment plans shall identify the selected control, accountable owner, target completion date, and required evidence of implementation.
- Residual risks shall be re-evaluated after implementation and at each scheduled review cycle.
- The Statement of Applicability shall be updated whenever a new control is adopted, an existing control is materially changed, or a control becomes not applicable.
