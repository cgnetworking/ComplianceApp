## 1. Purpose
Define security requirements for supplier selection, contracting, oversight, cloud usage, and ongoing assurance.

## 2. Scope
Applies to the third-party manufacturer, colocation provider, Azure, Microsoft 365, GitLab, Atlassian, CrowdStrike, Fortigate support arrangements, and any other suppliers that store, process, transmit, or support in-scope information or systems.

## 3. Authority and Compliance
This policy is issued under the authority of the CTO. Compliance with this document is mandatory for all personnel, contractors, and third parties within its scope. Non-compliance shall be addressed through management action, corrective action, contractual enforcement, or disciplinary measures as applicable.

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities
- The CTO is the document approver and provides executive authorization for this document.
- The Head of IT is the document owner and is responsible for implementation, maintenance, and periodic review.
- Managers shall ensure that personnel within their areas of responsibility comply with the requirements of this document.
- All workers, contractors, and third parties in scope shall comply with the requirements defined in this document.

## 5. Policy Requirements
### 5.1 Supplier Governance
- Suppliers shall be risk-assessed before onboarding and categorized based on the sensitivity and criticality of the service.
- Contracts shall address information security expectations, confidentiality, incident notification, right-to-audit or equivalent assurance, business continuity support, and exit requirements.
- Changes in supplier services, certifications, security posture, or critical dependencies shall be reviewed and documented.

### 5.2 Cloud Security
- Use of Azure and other cloud services shall follow documented ownership, configuration, access, logging, backup, and exit planning requirements.
- Shared responsibility boundaries shall be documented for each material cloud or hosted service.
- Current supplier certifications may be used as assurance input, but they do not replace internal due diligence.

### 5.3 Supplier Governance Requirements
- Due diligence shall evaluate supplier criticality, information access, service dependency, certifications, security commitments, incident notification obligations, and exit dependencies.
- Contracts shall define security responsibilities, confidentiality, change notification, continuity expectations, breach reporting, and evidence rights appropriate to risk.
- Critical suppliers shall be reviewed at least annually and after material service, ownership, security, or compliance changes.

### 5.4 ICT Supply Chain and Cloud Requirements
- Cloud and hosted services shall have documented ownership, configuration standards, logging expectations, backup responsibilities, and exit considerations.
- Shared responsibility boundaries shall be documented for Azure, SaaS providers, the colocation provider, and the hardware manufacturer.
- For third-party manufactured hardware, supplier assurance shall address secure production, component provenance where available, firmware or software integrity expectations where applicable, shipping controls, and defect or recall notification processes.

Any exception, deviation, or temporary waiver from this document shall be documented, risk-assessed, approved by the Head of IT, and authorized by the CTO before implementation unless emergency conditions prevent prior approval.

## 8. Review and Maintenance
This document shall be reviewed at least annually and whenever significant changes occur to the business, technology stack, supplier model, organizational structure, or applicable legal and contractual obligations.
