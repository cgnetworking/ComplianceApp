## 1. Purpose
Define the governance framework for the ISMS, including policy approval, security objectives, roles, oversight, compliance monitoring, and management review.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all workers, information assets, SaaS platforms, Azure resources, GitLab repositories, collaboration tools, company-managed endpoints, and company-owned systems hosted in the colocation facility.

## 3. Authority and Compliance
This policy is issued under the authority of the CTO. Compliance with this document is mandatory for all personnel, contractors, and third parties within its scope. Non-compliance shall be addressed through management action, corrective action, contractual enforcement, or disciplinary measures as applicable.

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities
- The CTO is the document approver and provides executive authorization for this document.
- The Head of IT is the document owner and is responsible for implementation, maintenance, and periodic review.
- Managers shall ensure that personnel within their areas of responsibility comply with the requirements of this document.
- All workers, contractors, and third parties in scope shall comply with the requirements defined in this document.

## 5. Policy Requirements
### 5.1 Governance Requirements
- The CTO shall approve the ISMS scope, policy suite, security objectives, risk treatment decisions, and annual management review outputs.
- The Head of IT shall operate the ISMS, maintain the risk register, coordinate evidence collection, and report control status to management.
- Security responsibilities shall be assigned in job descriptions, procedures, and system ownership records.
- Conflicting duties shall be segregated for code deployment, infrastructure administration, financial approval, and access approval where practical.
- Management shall ensure workers understand and follow security obligations relevant to their roles.

### 5.2 External Coordination
- The organization shall maintain contact points for law enforcement, regulators when applicable, incident response support, cyber insurance, and relevant security communities.
- Threat intelligence from vendors, CERTs, mailing lists, and trusted advisories shall be reviewed and incorporated into defensive actions.

### 5.3 Assurance Activities
- The ISMS shall be independently reviewed through internal audit or equivalent independent assessment at least annually.
- Compliance with policies, procedures, and standards shall be monitored through audits, reviews, sampling, and corrective actions.
- Documented operating procedures shall exist for recurring operational tasks that affect security, resilience, or evidence generation.

### 5.4 Policy Administration and Communication
- Policies shall be approved before issue, published in a controlled repository, communicated to affected personnel, and acknowledged where required.
- Policy owners shall review documents for continued suitability following material business, threat, supplier, technology, or legal change.
- Management shall ensure conflicts between security, operational, and business objectives are resolved through documented risk decisions rather than informal exceptions.

### 5.5 Oversight and Assurance
- A control ownership matrix shall be maintained for all Annex A controls, key systems, and critical suppliers.
- Corrective actions from incidents, audits, risk reviews, exercises, and management reviews shall be tracked to closure with due dates and accountable owners.
- Security requirements shall be included in project gates, procurement decisions, architecture approvals, and operational handovers.

## 7. Exceptions and Deviations
Any exception, deviation, or temporary waiver from this document shall be documented, risk-assessed, approved by the Head of IT, and authorized by the CTO before implementation unless emergency conditions prevent prior approval.

## 8. Review and Maintenance
This document shall be reviewed at least annually and whenever significant changes occur to the business, technology stack, supplier model, organizational structure, or applicable legal and contractual obligations.
