## 1. Purpose
Protect company assets hosted in the colocation facility, shipment office, and remote environments from unauthorized physical access, theft, damage, and environmental threats.

## 2. Scope
Applies to the shipping office, remote working locations, company laptops, portable assets, storage media, and company-owned infrastructure installed at the colocation facility.

## 3. Authority and Compliance
This policy is issued under the authority of the CTO. Compliance with this document is mandatory for all personnel, contractors, and third parties within its scope. Non-compliance shall be addressed through management action, corrective action, contractual enforcement, or disciplinary measures as applicable.

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities
- The CTO is the document approver and provides executive authorization for this document.
- The Head of IT is the document owner and is responsible for implementation, maintenance, and periodic review.
- Managers shall ensure that personnel within their areas of responsibility comply with the requirements of this document.
- All workers, contractors, and third parties in scope shall comply with the requirements defined in this document.

## 5. Policy Requirements
### 5.1 Facility Security
- Physical security perimeters and entry controls shall protect company-controlled spaces and assets.
- The shipping office shall remain locked when unattended and access shall be limited to authorized personnel with a business need.
- The colocation provider shall supply physical entry controls, surveillance, environmental protections, utilities, and monitored secure areas under contract.

### 5.2 Equipment Protection
- Company equipment shall be protected against theft, tampering, power instability, heat, fire, and other environmental threats appropriate to the location.
- Portable assets used off-premises shall remain under user control or stored in a secure location.
- Storage media shall be inventoried when used, protected during transport, and sanitized or destroyed before disposal or reuse.

### 5.3 Maintenance and Disposal
- Equipment maintenance shall be performed by authorized parties and records shall be retained for material repairs or datacenter interventions.
- Cabling, supporting utilities, and environmental safeguards for the colocation facility shall be managed through supplier controls and reviewed through supplier assurance.
- Retired devices and media shall be wiped or physically destroyed using approved methods.

### 5.4 Office and Colocation Security Requirements
- Access lists for the shipping office and colocation visits shall be approved, periodically reviewed, and updated promptly when roles change.
- Visitors and third-party maintenance personnel shall be supervised or controlled according to site rules and documented work authorizations.
- Physical keys, access cards, and provider-issued credentials shall be issued sparingly and recovered when no longer required.

### 5.5 Equipment Protection and Disposal Controls
- Portable assets shall not be left unattended in unsecured locations, checked baggage, or visible in vehicles except during unavoidable short-duration transport.
- Storage media and retired equipment shall be sanitized or destroyed using approved methods before disposal, warranty return, reassignment, or reuse.
- Environmental dependencies for datacenter-hosted equipment, including power, cooling, fire protection, and cabling protection, shall be validated through supplier assurance and planned maintenance records.
